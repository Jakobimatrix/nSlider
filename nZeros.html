The Zeros of a Polynomial

This slider allows you to adjust the zeros of a polynomial:
The magenta one and the red one are the zeros. The cyan one is a multiplying factor. You can add more zeros with the button below the slider. You can also remove zeros by right clicking on them red ones.

Observe how the handles can cross each other, but they can not have the same value. (I don't know why I demonstrated the feature in this example, because it is completely valid to have a double root.)
Awwe at the cool ticks generated by NSlider and the neat tooltip showing the value of each handle.
Have you seen, that you can only delete the red zeros?
Be amazed by the dynamic growth of the slider range when you move the handles to the edges.
